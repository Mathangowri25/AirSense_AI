**AQI Analysis Report**

**1. Main Causes**

Based on the AQI reading of 155.6 and pollutant levels, the primary contributors to poor air quality are:
- High PM10 levels (107.13µg/m³), indicating significant particulate matter in the air.
- Moderate to high PM2.5 levels (64.4µg/m³), which can further exacerbate respiratory issues.
Additionally, the moderate levels of NO2 (41.93µg/m³) and CO (2.538ppm) contribute to the overall poor AQI.

**2. Health Effects**

The AQI of 155.6 and associated pollutant levels pose a moderate risk to residents with pre-existing respiratory issues. Health effects may include:
- Increased risk of respiratory problems, such as coughing and shortness of breath.
- Potential exacerbation of existing cardiovascular and lung conditions.
- Discomfort or irritation to eyes, nose, and throat.
- Increased risk of respiratory infections.

**3. Safety Precautions**

Residents are advised to take the following precautions:
- Avoid outdoor activities, particularly for sensitive groups such as children, older adults, and people with pre-existing health conditions.
- Stay indoors with air conditioning or air purifiers when possible.
- Keep windows and doors closed to prevent pollutants from entering homes.
- Use masks with a filter rating of HEPA (High-Efficiency Particulate Air) when outdoors.

**4. Pollution Sources**

The following are potential sources of pollutants contributing to poor air quality:
- Vehicle emissions (NO2, CO, PM2.5, and PM10).
- Industrial activities (PM2.5, PM10, and SO2).
- Agricultural burning or other open-burning activities (PM2.5 and PM10).
- Construction or demolition activities (PM2.5 and PM10).

**5. Improvement Suggestions**

To improve air quality, residents and authorities can implement the following measures:
- Promote the use of public transportation or carpooling.
- Encourage the adoption of electric or hybrid vehicles.
- Implement emission-reducing measures in industrial facilities and agricultural activities.
- Enforce regulations against open burning or construction activities that release pollutants into the air.
